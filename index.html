The Life of Our Shoes

From raw material to landfill and everything inbetween

01
Raw Materials

Born from the Earth

A single pair of sneakers can contain over 65 different materials — rubber from Southeast Asian plantations, synthetic leather derived from crude oil, cotton, polyester, foam, and metal eyelets sourced from multiple continents.

65+ Materials per pair
~13kg CO₂ to produce materials
Environmental impact
🌿
02
Manufacturing

Assembled by Hand

Most shoes are still assembled by hand in factories across Asia. A single shoe can involve 360+ individual steps. Factories consume significant energy and produce wastewater from dyeing and chemical treatment processes.

360+ Assembly steps
~8kg Additional CO₂
Environmental impact
🏭
03
Transport & Retail

Crossing the Globe

Shoes travel an average of 15,000 km by sea freight before reaching a store. Then another leg by truck to warehouses and retail. Packaging adds further material waste, though some brands are moving to recycled materials.

15,000km Average journey
~2kg Transport CO₂
Environmental impact
🚢
04
Use & Care

On Your Feet

The average shoe is worn for just 1–3 years before being discarded. Washing trainers in a machine releases microplastics into waterways. Proper care — hand washing, resoling, and repair — can double a shoe's lifespan.

1–3 yrs Average lifespan
~1,900 Microplastic fibres per wash
💡 Tip: Use a laundry bag designed to capture microplastics when washing shoes.
👣
05
End of Life

The Final Step

Around 300 million pairs of shoes are thrown away each year in the US alone. Most end up in landfill where synthetic materials take up to 1,000 years to decompose. However, recycling programmes and bio-based materials are creating new paths forward.

300M Pairs discarded/year (US)
1,000 yrs To decompose in landfill
Environmental impact
♻️ Better choice: Donate, repair, or look for brand take-back recycling schemes.
